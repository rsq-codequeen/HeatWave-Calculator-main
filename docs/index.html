Application of Forier Series in the Heat Equation

Explore how Forier Series helps solve and approximate solutions in the heatwave (heat conduction) equation—crucial in thermal physics and engineering.

What is the Forier Series?

A Fourier series represents a periodic function as a sum of sine and cosine functions, essentially breaking down a complex wave into its fundamental and harmonic frequencies.
a0 = (1/l) ∫-ll f(x) dx
an = (1/l) ∫-ll f(x) cos(nπx/l) dx,   n = 0, 1, 2, ...
bn = (1/l) ∫-ll f(x) sin(nπx/l) dx,  n = 1, 2, ...
f(x) = a0/2 + ∑∞n=1 [ an cos(nπx/l) + bn sin(nπx/l) ]

  1. f(x): This represents the function you want to express in terms of sine and cosine functions.
  2. a₀/2: Constant term (average value over one period), normalized by 1/2.
  3. Σ: Summation symbol, adding terms from n=1 to ∞.
  4. aₙ cos(nωx): Cosine component with amplitude aₙ for the nth harmonic
  5. bₙ sin(nωx): Sine component with amplitude bₙ for the nth harmonic.
This approximation becomes more accurate with more terms,
and it’s especially useful in physics and engineering to solve differential equations analytically or numerically.

What is the Heat Equation?

Also known as the heat or diffusion equation, it describes how heat (or variation in temperature) diffuses through a medium over time. The 1D form is:
∂u/∂t = α ∂²u/∂x²
Here, u(x,t) is temperature, and α is thermal diffusivity. It’s widely used in meteorology, engineering, and environmental science to model temperature spread.

  1. ∂u/∂t: This term represents the rate of change of the quantity \( u \) with respect to time \( t \). In the context of heat, \( u \) could represent the temperature at a given position.
  2. ∂²u/∂x²: This term represents the second derivative of \( u \) with respect to position \( x \). It reflects how the temperature changes over space, specifically how the rate of change of temperature is itself changing, which is a measure of the curvature of the temperature profile.
  3. α (alpha): This is a constant that is typically called the thermal diffusivity. It dictates how fast heat will spread through the material. A higher value of \( α \) means that heat diffuses more quickly.

the heat equation states that the rate of change of temperature at a point is proportional to the curvature of the temperature distribution at that point, scaled by the constant ( α ). This means that if a point is hotter or cooler than its neighbors, it will change temperature over time until it approaches a more uniform distribution, showing the natural flow of heat in a medium.

Application of Forier Series in Heatwave Equation

The Forier Series helps solve the heatwave equation numerically. For instance, we can predict temperature at the next time step t + Δt using:

  1. Hot Water Pipe
    Phenomenon: When hot water flows inside a pipe, the pipe’s surface also becomes hot. Over time, this heat spreads through the pipe and to the air outside.
    How it works: Heat moves from the hot water to the pipe wall and then slowly moves out to the cooler outside air. This causes the temperature inside the pipe and around it to change over time.
  2. Mobile Phone Heating
    Phenomenon: When you use a mobile phone for a long time or charge it, it starts to heat up because the battery and processor produce heat.
    How it works: The heat generated inside the phone spreads through its parts, making the phone warm. If the heat is not controlled, it can damage the phone or make it uncomfortable to hold.
Role of Heat Equation: The heat equation helps us understand how heat moves through the pipe material and through inside the phone over time and distance. Role of Fourier Series: Because the heat equation is difficult to solve directly, we use Fourier series to break the temperature pattern into simple waves. Adding these waves together gives the full picture of how temperature changes in the pipe.
